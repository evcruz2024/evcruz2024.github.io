Solution to Poverty - Draft

Poverty is a major problem in the Philippines. Everywhere you go, there are adults and children who are just living on the streets. According to www.pna.gov.ph, out of 109,581,078 Filipinos, there are approximately 21,697,053 who are in poverty. This is about 19.8% of the population, which is almost ⅕. In addition to this, there are about 250,000 children without a home. This statistic comes from borgenproject.org. According to research, impoverished people have a lower life expectancy than people who don’t live in poverty. My proposal to solve this huge problem is stated in the following paragraphs.

A solution for poverty that I came up with is to sell these people afflicted with poverty to the rich. Specifically, the bones and other useful body parts may be sold to the rich. These bodies will then be used to build houses, skyscrapers, and other useful infrastructure can be built from these body parts. These infrastructure might be weak because malnutrition is a big problem among impoverished people,but, when infused with several strengthening materials such as iron, copper, and phosphorus, it becomes sturdy. Also, if these bones are fused to make some sort of a block or brick, it may be suitable for construction purposes...

Having this kind of community is not only the people’s fault but also the government neglecting it at the early stage, they did not take actions to it and resulted to what we have now.
